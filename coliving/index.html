Location

The house is located on the Costa del Sol in the tranquil town of Benajarafe. The town is very conveniently located, within half an hour drive of Malaga, closer than some of the popular holiday destinations as Marbella or Fuengirola, but with a fraction of the tourists. The town is very small and quiet and it is the perfect place to enjoy a slow pace of life and relax.

Things to do

Malaga

30 min drive

A vibrant city blending history and modernity. Explore its charming old town, impressive cathedral, and beautiful port area. Malaga also offers great museums, delicious local cuisine, and an inviting urban beach just minutes from the center.

Frigiliana

30 min drive

Often voted the prettiest village in Andalusia, Frigiliana offers a maze of whitewashed houses and cobbled streets.
Its Moorish quarter, artisan shops, and scenic viewpoints make it ideal for a relaxing day trip.

Torcal de Antequera

1 hour drive

A unique nature reserve famous for its surreal limestone formations shaped over millions of years. The hiking trails lead through a dramatic landscape ideal for photography, wildlife spotting, and enjoying nature.

Caves of Nerja

30 min drive

A spectacular series of enormous caverns stretching nearly five kilometers, home to one of the world’s largest stalactites. Guided routes illuminate the geological formations and prehistoric paintings that make these caves globally significant.

Benajarafe Beach

10 min walk

Your local beach, known for its dark sand, calm atmosphere, and pleasant waves. It’s perfect for relaxing walks, sunset views, and enjoying beachfront chiringuitos with fresh seafood.

Torre del Mar

15 min drive

A lively coastal town with one of Europe’s longest and nicest promenades, ideal for cycling or seaside strolls. The area offers great restaurants, wide beaches, and a relaxed holiday atmosphere year-round.

Sierras de Tejada

30 min drive

A stunning natural park forming a rugged border between Malaga and Granada, known for its dramatic peaks. It’s a paradise for hikers, offering trails with panoramic views, rich vegetation, and abundant wildlife.

La Sierra Nevada

2 hour drive

A dramatic mountain range offering world-class skiing in winter and exceptional hiking routes in summer. The region boasts unique flora and fauna, picturesque alpine villages, and Spain’s highest peak: Mulhacén.

Granada

1 hour 30 min drive

A city steeped in Moorish history, famously home to the Alhambra, one of the world’s greatest architectural treasures. Its narrow streets, vibrant tapas culture, and scenic viewpoints make it a must-visit destination.

Caminito del Rey

1 hour 15 min drive

Once known as the world’s most dangerous path, the Caminito del Rey is now a safe yet thrilling walkway along steep gorge walls.
The route offers breathtaking canyon views and a unique adventure into Andalusia’s wild interior landscapes.

Ronda

1 hour 30 min drive

A mountaintop city set dramatically above a massive gorge, connected by the iconic Puente Nuevo bridge.
Ronda combines breathtaking scenery with rich cultural heritage, making it one of Spain’s most romantic destinations.

Mijas

45 min drive

A classic Andalusian white village nestled in the mountains, offering breathtaking views over the Costa del Sol. Wander its narrow streets, enjoy artisan shops, and visit its small but charming bullring and historical landmarks.
